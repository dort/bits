  Combinatorics
  - permutations(iterable, r) — P(n,r) orderings
  - combinations(iterable, r) — C(n,r) subsets
  - combinations_with_replacement(iterable, r) — multisets

  Infinite Iterators
  - count(start, step) — arithmetic sequence
  - cycle(iterable) — periodic repetition
  - repeat(value, n) — constant sequence

  Accumulation
  - accumulate(iterable, func) — running totals/scans (partial sums, products, max)

  Filtering
  - takewhile / dropwhile — stop/start at predicate boundary
  - filterfalse — complement of filter
  - compress — boolean mask selection
  - islice — lazy slicing of infinite iterators

  Grouping & Chaining
  - groupby — consecutive grouping (requires sorted input!)
  - chain / chain.from_iterable — concatenation/flattening
  - zip_longest — zip with padding

  Mathematical Applications
  - Power set construction
  - Perfect matchings generator
  - Prime sieve using count
  - Dot product with starmap
  - Polynomial evaluation (Horner's method) with accumulate

  Your friend will appreciate that these are all lazy iterators — they don't materialize the full sequence in memory, making them efficient for large/infinite sequences.
